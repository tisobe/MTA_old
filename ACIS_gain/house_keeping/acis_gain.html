ACIS Gain Plots

ACIS gains were computed with following steps (see C. Grant memo:ACIS Gain @ -120 C for more discussion ):

Example Plot: CCD 3 Gain

  • All ACIS calibration event 1 files are extracted from Achieve, except squeegee files.
  • Each data is compared with focal temperature, and only parts with focal temperature equal to or lower than -119.7 C before May 2006, and -119.0 C from May 2006, are extracted out.
  • From these data, only ccdy ≤ 20 (first 20 raw of a CCD) and grade 0, 2, 3, 4, and 6 are extracted.
  • The extracted pha data are combined for each month to increase fitting accuracies.
  • A pulse height distribution is created from this data, and fit Lorentzian profiles to Al K-alpha (1486.7 eV), Ti K-alpha (4510.84 eV), and Mn K-alpha (5898.75 eV) to find peak postions in ADU.
  • A straight line is fit between three peak position in ADU and eV, and a slope (Gain ADU/eV), and an intercept (Offset ADU) were found.

For a gain plot, an x axis is in unit of year, and a y axis is in ADU/eV, the range for the y axis is 0.006 for all the plots so that we can compare the general trend among the plots. Similarly for offset plots, a y axis is in ADU, and the range for the y axis is 13 for all the plots. The slopes are either ADU/eV per year, or ADU per year.

Data entries are: Date, Obsid, starting time in seconds from 1998 Jan 1, end time in seconds from 1998 Jan 1, Al K-alpha position in ADU, Ma K-alpha in ADU, Ti K-alpha in ADU, slopes (ADU/eV), errors for the slopes, intercepts (ADU), and errors for the intercepts.
