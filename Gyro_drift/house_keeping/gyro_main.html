Grating Motion and Gyro Drift Rate Monitoring

On this page, we trends the effect of a grating motion on the stability of the spacecraft. The following steps are taken to produce the trending plots.

  • Fit a polynomial line on gyro drift rates data.
  • Take the difference between the fitted line and data points of before, during, and after of each grating motion.
  • Compute the average and it standard deviation.

For more detail descriptions of the analysis, please check memo.

If the standard deviations are significantly different among these three periods, the grating motion actually affects the stability of the spacecraft, and if the scattering of the standard deviations is getting larger, it indicates the grating motions affect the stability of the spacecraft more and more with time.

The following example plots show time trends of the standard deviations. The first one is before the grating motion started, the second is the during the motion, and the last one is the after the motion completed. The next three plots are ratios of standard deviations of before/during, after/during, and before/after of the grating motion.
